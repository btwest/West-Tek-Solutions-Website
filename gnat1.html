Mission Description

Mission: Gnat represents West-Tek Solutions' inaugural venture into 3D-printed rocketry, marking our transition from concept to launch pad. While our primary objective is straightforward—launch, recover, and prepare for relaunch of our first fully 3D-printed rocket—this mission serves as our foundational entry point into practical aerospace operations. The Gnat rocket, powered by an A34t single-use motor, represents proven, manageable technology that allows us to focus on mastering the essential elements: launch procedures, safety protocols, recovery operations, and post-flight analysis.

This mission is fundamentally about building our knowledge base and operational framework. As our first foray into rocketry, every aspect—from pre-flight preparation to post-recovery inspection—is designed to establish supply chains, refine checklists, test our 3D printing techniques under real-world conditions, and build the institutional knowledge that comes only from hands-on experience. Each challenge we encounter and lesson we learn directly feeds into our next-generation designs. This is where bigger and better rockets begin—not with the most advanced technology we can imagine, but with the solid foundation of experience we gain by taking our first steps into the sky.

Technical Specifications

  • Engine: Estes A3-4T Single-Use Motor
  • Estimated Altitude: 300-400 feet
  • Recovery: Tumble
  • Payload: Green Alien Toy 👽

Mission Objectives

🎯 Primary Objective

Successful launch, flight, and recovery of our first fully 3D-printed rocket with minimal damage to enable relaunch capability

🔬 Secondary Objective

Establish and validate complete launch procedures, safety protocols, and operational checklists for future West-Tek rocket missions

📊 Data Collection

Document 3D printing performance under flight stress, recovery system effectiveness, and identify supply chain and procedural improvements for next-generation designs
