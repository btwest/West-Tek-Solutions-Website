Mission: GNAT 1

UPCOMING

West-Tek Solutions is targeting Sunday, September 7 for launch of our inaugural Gnat 1 rocket from the West Family Launch Site. Liftoff is targeted for 4:00 PM EST.

This will be the first flight for our 3D-Printed Gnat Rocket, powered by a 1/2 A3-4T single-use motor. Following liftoff, the rocket will deploy its recovery system and return to the launch site for inspection and potential relaunch preparation.

Mission: Gnat represents West-Tek Solutions' foundational entry into 3D-printed rocketry, marking our transition from concept to launch pad. While our primary objective is straightforward—launch, recover, and prepare for relaunch—this mission serves as our critical first step into practical aerospace operations. The Gnat rocket represents proven, manageable technology that allows us to focus on mastering the essential elements: launch procedures, safety protocols, recovery operations, and post-flight analysis.

This mission is fundamentally about building our knowledge base and operational framework. As our inaugural venture into rocketry, every aspect—from pre-flight preparation to post-recovery inspection—is designed to establish supply chains, refine checklists, test our 3D printing techniques under real-world conditions, and build the institutional knowledge that comes only from hands-on experience. Each challenge we encounter and lesson we learn directly feeds into our next-generation designs. This is where bigger and better rockets begin—not with the most advanced technology we can imagine, but with the solid foundation of experience we gain by taking our first steps into the sky.

Mission Summary

SUCCESS

West-Tek Solutions successfully completed our inaugural rocket launch on September 7, 2025, under partly cloudy skies with temperatures in the mid to high 60s°F and relatively windy conditions. We launched four GNAT 1 rockets, each powered by 1/2 A3-4T motors, with all four achieving successful liftoffs and flight profiles. Most importantly, we successfully recovered our very first launch vehicle in perfect condition—a fantastic omen for West-Tek's entry into rocketry.

While we only recovered one of the four rockets (a 25% recovery rate), this actually represents an impressive achievement given that GNAT rockets are infamous for being difficult to recover. The recovered GNAT 1 showed no meaningful structural damage, with the PLA plastic construction performing excellently under flight stresses. Given its historic significance as our first successful launch and recovery, we've decided to retire this particular vehicle for posterity.

Overall, we're extremely happy with this launch and learned tremendous amounts about the entire process. The primary objective of GNAT 1 wasn't just to fly a rocket, but to master everything that goes into launching rockets—from pre-flight preparation and safety protocols to recovery operations and post-flight analysis. Through this mission, we've established our operational procedures, validated our 3D printing techniques under real-world conditions, and built the institutional knowledge that comes only from hands-on experience. Because of everything we learned during GNAT 1, we feel strongly positioned and ready to tackle future launches of bigger and better rockets.
